Certifications

Certified CyberDefender (CCD)

CCD is a vendor-neutral, hands-on cybersecurity training and certification. It is designed to prepare the next generation of SOC analysts, security blue teams, threat hunters, and DFIR professionals, building skills across multiple domains such as SecOps Fundamentals, Perimeter Defense, Incident Response, Digital Forensics, Threat Hunting and Malware Analysis.

CompTIA Security+

Earners of the CompTIA Security+ certification have the knowledge and skills necessary to perform core security functions required of any cybersecurity role. CompTIA Security+ professionals know how to identify and address potential threats, attacks and vulnerabilities and they have established techniques in risk management, risk mitigation, threat management and intrusion detection.

EC-Council SOC Analyst Certified (CSA)

The Certified SOC Analyst has an in-depth understanding of various attack methodologies, to collaborating with the IRTs in case of an incident. A certified member has adequate knowledge to bring people, process and technology together and provide a comprehensive skill set to manage a robust security operation centre with enhanced incident detection and response capabilities.

Blue Team Level 1 (BTL1)

Earners of the Blue Team Level 1 Certification have showcased the practical ability to defend networks and systems from cyber threats through technical and hands-on defensive cybersecurity training. The have knowledgable and ability across 5 security operations domains which include Phishing Analysis, Digital Forensics, Threat Intelligence, SIEM, and Incident Response. Beta Testers have played a crucial part in the development of BTL1 by providing structured review and feedback.

Junior Penetration Tester (eJPT)

Assessment Methodologies, Web Application Penetration Test, Host & Networking Penetration Testing, Host & Networking Auditing

Google CyberSecurity Professional

Those who earn the Google Cybersecurity Certificate, developed by Google, have demonstrated their competence in foundational cybersecurity tactics. Through hands-on activities and assessments, graduates develop proficiency in identifying common risks and vulnerabilities and using Python, Linux, SQL, SIEM tools, and Intrusion Detection Systems to mitigate threats, preparing them for entry-level roles in cybersecurity.

Cisco Certified CCNA

Earners of Cisco CCNA - Implementing and Administering Cisco Solutions (CCNA) have demonstrated knowledge and skills related to network fundamentals, network access, IP connectivity, IP services, security fundamentals, and automation and programmability.
